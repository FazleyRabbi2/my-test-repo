Chandrayaan-3

Chandrayaan-3 (transl. Moon-craft, pronunciation (help·info)) is the third Indian lunar exploration mission under the Indian Space Research Organisation's (ISRO) Chandrayaan programme. It consists of a lander named Vikram and a rover named Pragyan, similar to those of the Chandrayaan-2 mission. The propulsion module carried the lander and rover configuration to lunar orbit in preparation for a powered descent by the lander. Chandrayaan-3 was launched on 14 July 2023. The lander and rover landed at the lunar south pole region on 23 August 2023 at 18:02 IST, making India the first country to successfully land a spacecraft near the lunar south pole and the fourth country to soft-land on the Moon.

Objectives

ISRO's mission objectives for the Chandrayaan-3 mission were:

  1. Getting a lander to land safely and softly on the surface of the Moon.
  2. Getting a lander to land safely and softly on the surface of the Moon.
  3. Conducting and observing experiments on the materials available on the lunar surface to better understand the composition of the Moon.

On 23 August 2023, as the lander approached the low point of its orbit, its four engines fired as a braking manoeuvre at 30 kilometres (19 mi) above the Moon's surface. After 11.5 minutes, the lander was 7.2 km (4.5 miles) above the surface; it maintained this altitude for about 10 seconds, then stabilized itself using eight smaller thrusters and rotated from a horizontal to a vertical position while continuing its descent.
